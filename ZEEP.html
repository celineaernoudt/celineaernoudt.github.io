ZEEP

sculpture / performance with Tim Choin

"Le savon" by Francis Ponge read by Irma Emmery

(2016)


During the performance you could see us, in our overalls, cutting and moulding the angular block into the general shape of soap. Mean- while, surrounded by smoke, you could listen to fragments of “Le savon”, a book by Francis Ponge.

The soap currently measures ca. 145 x 40 x 80 centimeters and weighs approximatly 350 kilo- gram.


Soap is a magic stone!

“A kind of stone, but a species that in nature doesn’t roll itself: it slips through your fingers and rather dissolves before your eyes, than by letting it wallow by the water. Then the game also consists of this, to grab it between your fin- gers and to provoke it with the right amount of water, in order to bring about a bulky, shiny reaction ... When we, on the other hand, lea-ve it in the water, it perishes from confusion.”

(Translated fragment from ‘Le savon’ by Francis Ponge)
